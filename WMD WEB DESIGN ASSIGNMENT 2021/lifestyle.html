Luxury Lifestyle

Paris is world famous for its unrivalled historical, cultural and architectural heritage. Paris is a unique city which attracts millions of visitors every year, as well as numerous expatriates who decide to make the city their home. With more than 136 museums, dozens of exhibitions, countless shows and a multitude of theatres, Paris is buzzing with life, and it is truly difficult to ever get bored in the city. Whether you are a history buff or an arts aficionado, Paris holds something for you which you will find nowhere else in the world.

Paris has plenty of resturants and cafes for expats of all palates. As a culinary capital of thw world Paris is famed for its gourmrnt eatries. The City's many patisseriesalso prove also prove that food as an art form is alive as well. The French are famous for their food, but those expates who've had their fill of croissants and creme brulee won't struggle to find restaurants offering international cuisinessuch as Chinese, Indian, Italian and Thai.

With a culture as a serious about its leisure time as the French, its no wonder that Paris has plenty to offer for those looking to spend the day outdoors. The city's climate is highly seasonal though, so the outdoor activities available oftendepend on the time of the day. Paris is home to a number of parks in which expats can enjoy leisurely strolls throughout the year. Parc de la Villete is a particular favourite, as throughout the summer months a large screen is put in front of which anyone can relax and watch classic films. The selection usually includes English as well as French cinema, and a screening can be a great place to enjoy picnic. While open-air movies are'nt available in Paris during the winter expats wanting to spend more time outdoors during frosty months, can instead enjoynumerous ice skating rinks around the city.
